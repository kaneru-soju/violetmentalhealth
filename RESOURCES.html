WHERE TO GET HELP

“I've never been good with asking for help; it seems risky, but at some point when things are really dicey, your stubbornness gives way to a certain form of humility that, after you get over yourself, feels liberating. I started to believe that the universe was conspiring to help me finish my house, sending people along at the right moment.”

Dee Williams

Hotlines (Global): Crisis/Suicide Hotlines

U.S. General Resources: The number 211 is available (not nationwide) to provide access to essential community resources. There is also a link at United Way 211 Community Resource Directory

U.S. LGBT+ Community: You are valid and you exist. The Trevor Project is a great resource when in need of help.

U.S. Community Resources: Another search directory that can be used when looking for resources, 1Degree

Students: More often than not, there are services available at school for free to help you when you are in need. Please do reach out.

Free Self-Care Resources: Being in touch with your body and mind can be hard, but here's some free resources... MyIntent

Social Resources: Communicate with those around you, find friends, and more (caution: don't forget about covid-19!)... MeetUp and Craigslist
